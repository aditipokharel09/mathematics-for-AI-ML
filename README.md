Day 1: 
The Core Concept:
A vector is an instruction for movement. It captures two ideas in one: Direction (where to go) and Amount (how far to go).
Geometric View (The Arrow)
1.Direction + Length: An arrow pointing somewhere.
2.Moveable: The arrow can start anywhere. Only the way it points and its length define the vector.
3.Basis Vectors: [1,0] and [0,1] are the "Lego bricks." Every other vector is just a combination of these two unit steps.

CS/AI View (The Data)
1.Feature Vectors: A vector is a digital "profile." Example: [180, 75] could represent [Height, Weight].
2.Order Matters: If you swap the numbers, you change the meaning of the data.
3.High Dimensions: An AI embedding is just an arrow in a space with hundreds of axes instead of just two.

Operations
1.Addition: Combining information. Putting arrows tip-to-tail to find a final destination.
2.Scaling: Adjusting intensity. Multiplying by 2 doubles the "strength" or importance. Multiplying by -1 flips the direction (the opposite idea).
